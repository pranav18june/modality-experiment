1. Your Task

In this study you will act as a supply chain planner. A disruption has occurred (e.g. a port delay, a supplier shutdown, a demand surge). An AI system has analysed the situation and generated a recommendation.

Your job is to decide how much to adjust two inventory parameters in response to the disruption:

Reorder Point (ROP)
The inventory level at which you place a new order. Increasing ROP means you order sooner, building a buffer against delays.
Safety Stock (SS)
Extra stock held as a cushion against demand or supply uncertainty. Increasing SS protects against stockouts but ties up capital.

For each scenario you will use two sliders to set percentage adjustments to ROP and SS relative to their current baseline values. The AI system will suggest values — you can accept, increase, or decrease them.

Example: If the baseline ROP is 200 units and you slide to +30%, the new ROP would be 260 units. If you slide to −10%, it becomes 180 units.
